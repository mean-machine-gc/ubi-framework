Ubi conceptualises information processing as an intricate network of decisions that comply with the General Decision Model (GDM). The GDM is a high-variance model, with no other practical utility than providing the theoretical basis for more constrained and canonical models. Constraints can then be relaxed on canonical models for systematic study of scenarios and real-life applications.

The GDM elaborates on the original H. Simon formulation, but — as for all references in this copy — it is left to the reader the task to untangle, if needed, where the original concept ends and the adaptation begins; the task of this copy is to explain the model as a final product without the burden of precise academic citations, while attempting to state as clearly as possible any due credits and attributions of original ideas.

## Decision Stages

In the GDM, an Actor is tasked to process information in the form of producing a Choice, hence a decision. For any decision, the Actor needs to engage with three distinct Decision Stages.

<aside class="margin-figure">
<div class="figure-label">Table 1 — Decision Stages</div>
<table>
  <thead>
    <tr><th>Decision Stage</th><th>Description</th></tr>
  </thead>
  <tbody>
    <tr><td>Information gathering</td><td>Gathering sufficient information for the decision</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Rationale design</td><td>Design a reference heuristics or algorithm for processing the gathered information</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Choice selection</td><td>Select and return the final choice for the decision</td></tr>
  </tbody>
</table>
</aside>

The decision process is an act of *bounded rationality* (H. Simon), where all three decision stages are not free but constrained. This makes global optimisations of decisions impossible, as the Actor always has a constrained supply of information; a constrained decision heuristic or algorithm; might have constraints on what choices are possible; the Actor always exercises its information processing capability under its own cognition constraints; and under the affordances of its own Agency, which determines what the Actor is and what it is able to do in the world or within a particular system.

## Decision Constraints

<aside class="margin-figure">
<div class="figure-label">Table 2 — Decision Constraints</div>
<table>
  <thead>
    <tr><th>Stage</th><th>Constraint</th><th>Implications</th></tr>
  </thead>
  <tbody>
    <tr><td>Information gathering</td><td><strong>Information Context</strong>: the limited set of information available to the Actor</td><td>The Actor never accesses the complete system's information space; rationality is bounded, resulting choices can only be <em>good enough</em> (<strong>bounded rationality</strong> and <strong>satisfycing</strong> in H. Simon's terms)</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Rationale design</td><td><strong>Procedure</strong>: a predefined heuristic, instructions, or algorithm</td><td>Decisions can be classified in <strong>Programmed</strong> (with a standard Procedure provided, or SOP) vs <strong>Non-Programmed</strong></td></tr>
    <tr><td>Choice selection</td><td><strong>Choice Space</strong>: a pre-defined set of possible Choices</td><td>Decisions can be classified in <strong>Closed Choice</strong> vs <strong>Open Choice</strong></td></tr>
    <tr><td>All stages</td><td><strong>Cognition Limits</strong>: the information processing limits of the Actor</td><td>Even with complete information, the decision remains a local optimisation problem</td></tr>
    <tr><td>All stages</td><td><strong>Agency</strong>: what the Actor is and what it can do</td><td>The GDM is not limited to humans — Actors can be humans, organisations, machines, AI-agents, as well as plants, animals, and rocks</td></tr>
  </tbody>
</table>
</aside>

Lastly, Decisions may or may not produce tangible effects in the real world — *i.e.* Side Effects — such as modifying existing information or creating new information.
